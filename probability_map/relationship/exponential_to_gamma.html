üìò Mathematical Relationship

If $X_1, X_2, \ldots, X_k$ are $k$ independent $\text{Exponential}(\lambda)$ random variables, their sum $Y = X_1 + \cdots + X_k$ follows a $\text{Gamma}(\alpha=k, \beta=\lambda)$ distribution.

PDF of Gamma: $$f(x; k, \lambda) = \frac{\lambda^k}{\Gamma(k)} x^{k-1} e^{-\lambda x}, \quad x > 0$$

Intuition: Each exponential represents a waiting time until an event. The sum of $k$ waiting times is the total time until $k$ events occur ‚Äî this is also called the Erlang distribution when $k$ is an integer.

Properties (by independence):
‚Ä¢ $E[Y] = k \cdot E[X_i] = k/\lambda$
‚Ä¢ $\text{Var}[Y] = k \cdot \text{Var}[X_i] = k/\lambda^2$

Special case: When $k = 1$, $\text{Gamma}(1, \lambda) = \text{Exponential}(\lambda)$.
