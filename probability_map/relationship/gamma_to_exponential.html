üìò Special Case Relationship

When the shape parameter $\alpha = 1$, the $\text{Gamma}(1, \beta)$ distribution is exactly the $\text{Exponential}(\beta)$ distribution.

PDF simplification: The Gamma PDF is: $$f(x; \alpha, \beta) = \frac{\beta^\alpha}{\Gamma(\alpha)} x^{\alpha-1} e^{-\beta x}$$ When $\alpha = 1$: $\Gamma(1) = 1$ and $x^0 = 1$, so $f(x) = \beta e^{-\beta x}$ ‚Äî the Exponential PDF.

Building block: The Exponential distribution is the "building block" of the Gamma ‚Äî summing $k$ independent $\text{Exp}(\beta)$ gives $\text{Gamma}(k, \beta)$.
