üìò Exponential Transform

If $X$ is normally distributed, then $Y = e^X$ follows a lognormal distribution. Equivalently, $\ln(Y)$ is normally distributed ‚Äî hence the name "lognormal".

PDF of Lognormal: $$f_Y(y) = \frac{1}{y\sigma\sqrt{2\pi}} \exp\left(-\frac{(\ln y - \mu)^2}{2\sigma^2}\right), \quad y > 0$$

Properties:
‚Ä¢ $E[Y] = e^{\mu + \sigma^2/2}$
‚Ä¢ $\text{Var}[Y] = (e^{\sigma^2} - 1) \cdot e^{2\mu + \sigma^2}$
‚Ä¢ $\text{Median}[Y] = e^\mu$
‚Ä¢ $\text{Mode}[Y] = e^{\mu - \sigma^2}$

Applications: Stock prices (geometric Brownian motion), income distributions, particle sizes ‚Äî any quantity that is the product of many positive random factors.
