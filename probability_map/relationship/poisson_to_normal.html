üìò Normal Approximation

For large $\lambda$, the $\text{Poisson}(\lambda)$ distribution is well-approximated by $\text{Normal}(\mu = \lambda, \sigma^2 = \lambda)$.

CLT Interpretation: A $\text{Poisson}(\lambda)$ can be viewed as the sum of $\lambda$ independent $\text{Poisson}(1)$ random variables: $$X = X_1 + X_2 + \cdots + X_\lambda, \quad X_i \overset{\text{iid}}{\sim} \text{Poisson}(1)$$ As $\lambda \to \infty$, the Central Limit Theorem implies this sum converges to a normal distribution.

Standardization: The standardized variable converges to standard normal: $$Z = \frac{X - \lambda}{\sqrt{\lambda}} \xrightarrow{d} N(0, 1) \quad \text{as } \lambda \to \infty$$

Rule of thumb: The approximation is reasonable when $\lambda \geq 10$, and good when $\lambda \geq 20$.
